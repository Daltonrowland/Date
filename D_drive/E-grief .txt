2. What The Model Actually Detects

E_GRIEF focuses on three layers:
	1.	Mind-load
	•	heavy language
	•	rumination
	•	regret loops
	•	“if only” style wording
	2.	Body-load
	•	long inactivity right after emotional input
	•	breathing patterns or heart rate if wearables exist later
	•	usage drop after intense sessions
	3.	Spirit-load
	•	high emotional intensity plus silence
	•	repeated visits to ExsAnonymous or Last Light
	•	longer Selah pauses than the user’s baseline
	•	language that points to loss, finality, or spiritual weight

Outcome:
The system outputs a single number:

Spiritual Depletion Index (SDI): 0 to 100

0 to 20: little or no grief
20 to 40: mild grief
40 to 70: active grief
70 to 100: deep grief / soul heavy

3. Input Signals and How They Feed The Score

You can explain this as a weighted model.
Here are the input categories and suggested weights.

A. Session Type (big signal)
	•	ExsAnonymous session completed: +15
	•	Last Light session completed: +25
	•	Both within 7 days: additional +10

B. Language and Content Markers

From text inside ExsAnonymous or Last Light:
	•	Loss/finality words (“gone”, “never again”, “goodbye”, “I miss you”, “I wish I could tell you”): +1 to +3 each, capped
	•	Self blame or regret (“my fault”, “should have”, “I ruined”, “if I had just”): +1 to +3 each, capped
	•	Spiritual wrestling (“God”, “why”, “why me”, “where were you”): +1 to +4 each, capped

Max from language block: about 30 points.

C. Stillness and Selah Behavior

This is where your “present but still” principle kicks in.
	•	Time spent in Selah Mode above the user’s baseline:
	•	0 to 20 percent above baseline: +3
	•	21 to 50 percent above baseline: +7
	•	more than 50 percent above baseline: +12
	•	Number of Selah uses within 24 hours after a grief session:
	•	1 Selah: +3
	•	2 to 3: +7
	•	4 or more: +10

D. Usage Pattern After Release

In the 24 to 72 hours after a heavy session:
	•	Log in less, look at nothing, just sit in Sanctuary or Selah: +5 to +10
	•	Opens the app, stares, then leaves with no action: +3 each time, capped
	•	Avoids matches, avoids messaging, only opens Sanctuary: +5

You do not need perfect accuracy.
You just need signal density to know “this is heavy.”

4. The Spiritual Depletion Index (SDI) Logic

You can define SDI like this in a simple way for dev:

SDI_raw = Session_Score
        + Language_Score
        + Selah_Score
        + Usage_Pattern_Score

SDI = min(100, SDI_raw)

Then turn it into tiers.
	•	0 to 20: Tier 0 “Stable”
	•	21 to 40: Tier 1 “Tender”
	•	41 to 70: Tier 2 “Active Grief”
	•	71 to 100: Tier 3 “Deep Grief”

This is your E_GRIEF_State.

5. What The System Actually Does In Each Tier

This is where Genesis OS stops being theory and starts acting like a guardian.

Tier 0: Stable
	•	Normal Sanctuary experience
	•	Selah offered but not pushed
	•	No special grief handling

Tier 1: Tender
	•	Sanctuary offers Selah gently after reflective sessions
	•	AI tone slightly softer
	•	Occasional quiet prompt like:
	•	“You do not have to rush your healing.”

Tier 2: Active Grief

Triggered often after ExsAnonymous or Last Light.

Actions:
	•	Auto Selah launch after any heavy session
	•	No push toward social features right away
	•	AI replies slower in tone, shorter, more grounded
	•	Whisper Window prompts are more about expression, not decisions
	•	Sanctuary shows a quiet card such as:
	•	“Grief is not something to fix. It is something to walk through.”

Tier 3: Deep Grief

This is where the system treats the user as fragile and sacred.

Actions:
	•	Sanctuary defaults to Stillness modes first, not stimulation
	•	Strong suggestion for Selah before any major interaction
	•	ExsAnonymous and Last Light offered as safe loops
	•	Notifications about matches and entertainment are reduced or paused
	•	AI presence is ultra soft. No pressure. No big calls to action.

Future layer:
If you ever integrate human support or partner therapists, this is the tier where the app might gently suggest:
	•	“If you ever want to talk to someone human about this, we can help you find that.”

Not forced. Just an option.
