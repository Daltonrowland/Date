⭐ What the Weighting Engine Does

In plain terms:

It takes all the signals from the Emotional Vault + behavior
→ turns them into pattern tags
→ uses those tags to raise or lower each archetype’s score
→ then outputs:
Primary Archetype, Secondary Influence, Minor Influence
	•	Shadow Pattern.

It’s the brain in the middle.

⭐ 3. Phase 2 – Apply Tag Weights to Archetypes

For each tag in the profile:
	•	Look up how that tag affects each archetype in your Weight Matrix
	•	Multiply tag_intensity × weight_value
	•	Add to that archetype’s running total

Conceptually:

Archetype_Score[archetype] 
  = Σ (Tag_Intensity[tag] * Weight[tag, archetype])

So if:
	•	Silent_Protection (6) gives +4 to Quiet Exit and +3 to Icebox,
→ Quiet Exit gets +24, Icebox gets +18
	•	Hyper_Responsibility (5) gives +5 to Fixer,
→ Fixer gets +25

After looping all tags, you end up with something like:

Analyzer: 22
QuietExit: 40
Fixer: 37
RomanticIdealist: 9
Performer: 5
Survivor: 27

Raw archetype pressures.

⭐ 4. Phase 3 – Normalize and Apply Depth Score

Now you adjust scores based on Depth Score and data completeness:
	•	Low Depth Score (0–20%) → apply a confidence penalty
	•	High Depth Score (60%+) → apply a confidence boost

Concept idea:

Normalized_Archetype_Score[a] = 
    Archetype_Score[a] * Depth_Confidence_Factor

Where:
	•	0–20% depth → factor ~ 0.6
	•	21–40% → ~ 0.8
	•	41–60% → 1.0
	•	61–80% → 1.1
	•	81–100% → 1.2

This says:

“The more of your truth we’ve seen, the more sure we are about what you are.”

⸻

⭐ 5. Phase 4 – Apply Reflection & Contradiction Adjustments

Here’s where the grown-up logic happens.

You add small boosts/penalties based on:
	•	Consistency clusters (like your Vulnerability Loop)
	•	Reflection patterns (Season of Healing behavior)
	•	Shadow tension (e.g. Chameleon, Scorekeeper)

Examples:
	•	If user is emotionally consistent across Vault + Test →
small boost to their top archetype.
	•	If they show high contradiction (e.g. “I’m independent” but cling hard in patterns) →
reduce confidence in archetypes that rely on self-awareness (Regulated, Translator),
boost shadows (Chameleon, Self-Saboteur).

This step tightens the emotional honesty vs self-image gap.

⸻

⭐ 6. Phase 5 – Select Primary, Secondary, Minor

Once all adjustments are applied:
	1.	Sort archetypes by final score.
	2.	Take the top 3:
	•	Top = Primary Archetype
	•	2nd = Secondary Influence
	•	3rd = Minor Influence

You might also store the underlying numbers for future logic.

Example final:

QuietExit: 82   → Primary
Fixer:     71   → Secondary
Survivor:  59   → Minor
Others:    < 50

Now the OS knows:

“You’re primarily Quiet Exit, with a strong Fixer side and a Survivor tone underneath.”

	•	Disappearing behavior
	•	Over-giving
	•	Love bombing
	•	Testing behavior
	•	Withholding affection

Example:
	•	Scorekeeper rises when:
	•	resentment
	•	tally-keeping
	•	“after all I’ve done for you” tone
	•	unspoken debts
	•	Stonewaller rises when:
	•	shutdown in conflict
	•	retreat instead of engagement
	•	refusal to communicate

The engine:

Shadow_Score[shadow] = Σ (Tag_Intensity[tag] * Shadow_Weight[tag, shadow])

Then choose:
	•	Top 1 = Primary Shadow
	•	Top 2 = Secondary Shadow (optional for advanced view)
