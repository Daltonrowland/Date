⭐ 1. Shared Framework (How all shadows are calculated)

For each shadow type:
	•	You define pattern tags that raise its score.
	•	Certain tags have more weight than others.
	•	You apply penalties if the user shows growth or regulation that conflicts with that shadow.
	•	Depth Score adjusts confidence (more data = more certainty).

Conceptually:

Shadow_Score[shadow] = Σ (Tag_Intensity[tag] * Shadow_Weight[tag, shadow])
Adjusted_Score = Shadow_Score * Depth_Confidence * Consistency_Factor

Then you:
	•	Normalize scores
	•	Rank them
	•	Take the top 1–2

Let’s map each shadow one by one.

🧠 2. SHADOW: THE MANIPULATOR

This is not “evil villain.” It’s:

“I pull strings emotionally to feel safe, powerful, or in control.”

A. High-Signal Tags
	•	Emotional_Lever_Pulling
	•	Guilt_Suggestion (“after all I’ve done…”)
	•	Ambiguous_Promising (hinting without committing)
	•	Mixed_Signal_Output (push/pull with intention)
	•	Attention_Drip_Feeding (breadcrumbing)
	•	Strategic_Vulnerability (oversharing to hook)

B. Detection Sources

From Vault + Answers:
	•	“I sometimes say things to see how they’ll react.”
	•	“If they pull back, I might exaggerate how I feel to pull them in.”
	•	“I know how to get people to chase me.”

From Behavior:
	•	Knocks multiple people then goes cold
	•	Comes back when partners move on
	•	Sends emotionally heavy DMs then disappears

C. Logic Indicators

Manipulator_Score goes up when:
	•	user admits any version of “I test people on purpose”
	•	patterns show intentionally stirring emotions to get a reaction
	•	there’s a mix of Chameleon + Scorekeeper + Love Bomber traits

It goes down when:
	•	there is high emotional accountability language
	•	Depth Score is high + consistent ownership of impact

🧱 3. SHADOW: THE STONEWALLER

“When things get real, I shut you out instead of engaging.”

A. High-Signal Tags
	•	Shutdown_Reflex
	•	Silent_Protection
	•	Door_Close_Impulse
	•	Emotional_Withdrawal
	•	Conflict_Avoidance
	•	Disappearing_After_Triggers

B. Detection Sources

Vault lines:
	•	“I go silent when I’m hurt.”
	•	“I just disappear instead of arguing.”
	•	“I shut down if I feel attacked or overwhelmed.”

Behavior:
	•	Seen in:
	•	read-and-vanish patterns
	•	Ghost Mode usage mid-conflict
	•	not replying after emotionally heavy exchanges

C. Logic Indicators

Stonewaller_Score rises when:
	•	Quiet Exit + Icebox + Analyzer patterns are strong
	•	withdrawal is used as control, not just regulation
	•	conflict repeatedly leads to silence with no repair
	•	user refuses to clarify or respond in high-intensity discussions

It decreases when:
	•	they describe circling back for repair
	•	there’s a pattern of “I cool off then communicate clearly.”

⸻

💥 4. SHADOW: THE LOVE BOMBER

“I go hard emotionally at the start to secure connection, then can’t sustain it.”

A. High-Signal Tags
	•	Intensity_Surge
	•	Fast_Attach
	•	Over_Promising
	•	Idealization_Pattern
	•	Fast_Merge (talking long-term very early)
	•	Grand_Gestures_Early

B. Detection Sources

Vault:
	•	“I fall fast.”
	•	“I say a lot upfront then feel trapped.”
	•	“I want to give them the world when I like them.”

Behavior:
	•	rapid message frequency spike
	•	heavy emotional language early
	•	saying “this feels different / I’ve never…” within days
	•	frequent “soulmate” language early

C. Logic Indicators

LoveBomber_Score rises when:
	•	Romantic Idealist + Phantom Seeker are strong
	•	there’s intensity without structural pacing
	•	high E_GRIEF + craving closeness
	•	impulsive emotional declarations

It decreases when:
	•	they express pacing awareness: “I used to, now I try to go slow”
	•	they demonstrate consistent follow-through and regulated pacing.

⸻

🎭 5. SHADOW: THE CHAMELEON

“I become who they need me to be so I don’t lose them.”

A. High-Signal Tags
	•	Self_Erasure
	•	Image_Shifting
	•	Approval_Seeking
	•	Identity_Borrowing
	•	Context_Switching_Persona
	•	Boundary_Disappearance

B. Detection Sources

Vault:
	•	“I adapt to what they like.”
	•	“I don’t really show my full self at first.”
	•	“I can be whoever they need.”
	•	“I tend to over-adjust to keep the peace.”

Behavior:
	•	different tone with different matches
	•	high Performer + Translator signals
	•	frequent contradictions in self-description across contexts

C. Logic Indicators

Chameleon_Score rises when:
	•	they repeatedly indicate shape-shifting to avoid loss or conflict
	•	Scorekeeper + Fixer + Performer patterns coexist
	•	their stated values conflict with their behavior across multiple answers

It decreases with:
	•	strong, consistent self-anchoring language
	•	saying “I’ve learned to stay myself even if it risks loss.”

🎯 6. SHADOW: THE SCOREKEEPER

“I keep a silent ledger of what I give vs what I get — and I use it.”

A. High-Signal Tags
	•	Resentment_Ledger
	•	“After_All_I’ve_Done” mindset
	•	Gift_Versus_Return focus
	•	Emotional_Tallying
	•	Quid_Pro_Quo_Resentment

B. Detection Sources

Vault:
	•	“I remember everything I did that they didn’t appreciate.”
	•	“I keep track of effort.”
	•	“I shut down when I feel like I’m doing more.”

Behavior:
	•	hard withdrawal after perceived imbalance
	•	subtle punishments when not matched
	•	emotionally transactional language

C. Logic Indicators

Scorekeeper_Score rises when:
	•	Fixer + Survivor + Phantom Seeker patterns show unbalanced effort
	•	they describe expecting emotional debt repayment
	•	they often feel “used” but keep giving and building quiet resentment

It decreases when:
	•	user expresses clean boundaries + letting go of “keeping score.”

📉 7. SHADOW: THE SELF-SABOTEUR

“I ruin what could work because my nervous system is calibrated to chaos, fear, or unworthiness.”

A. High-Signal Tags
	•	Chaos_Seeking
	•	Exit_Before_Intimacy
	•	“Too_Good_To_Be_True” reflex
	•	Comfort_With_Dysfunction
	•	Fear_Of_Being_Seen
	•	Testing_Beyond_Reason

B. Detection Sources

Vault:
	•	“When it gets real, I find a reason to pull away.”
	•	“Healthy feels boring sometimes.”
	•	“I pick fights when I feel too close.”
	•	“I don’t trust good things.”

Behavior:
	•	sabotaging when connection deepens
	•	disrupting stable patterns
	•	flirting elsewhere when it’s going well

C. Logic Indicators

SelfSaboteur_Score rises when:
	•	Survivor + Phantom + Romantic Idealist are strong
	•	relationship history shows repeated self-initiated breakage
	•	fear of receiving actually safe love

It decreases when:
	•	user acknowledges this pattern and describes new corrective behavior.

⭐ 8. Cross-Shadow Interaction Logic

Some shadows travel together:
	•	Chameleon + Scorekeeper → “I change for you and resent you for it.”
	•	Love Bomber + Self-Saboteur → “I rush in, then destroy it.”
	•	Manipulator + Stonewaller → “I provoke, then disappear.”

Your engine should:
	•	detect multiple high scores
	•	cluster them into Shadow Profiles like:
	•	“Soft Manipulator”
	•	“Silent Retaliator”
	•	“Chaotic Romantic”

But in the app, you present:
	•	Primary Shadow
	•	Secondary Shadow (optional in advanced view / premium)

⸻

⭐ 9. Risk Tiers

Each shadow can be classified in risk tiers:
	•	Tier 1 – Mild / Emerging
behavior is present but low-intensity
	•	Tier 2 – Active
behavior is recurring and impactful
	•	Tier 3 – High Impact
behavior is strong, repeated, and emotionally dangerous

This is determined by:
	•	Shadow_Score thresholds
	•	consistency of tags over time
	•	impact descriptions in Vault (how much damage they’ve done)

This lets you later:
	•	warn match engine
	•	adjust Knock™ rules
	•	alter Sanctuary prompts
	•	protect users.

⭐ 10. Shadow + Depth Score Integration

Low Depth Score =
Still forming, more guesswork.
System stays humble.

High Depth Score =
User has confessed real patterns.
Shadow detection becomes VERY accurate.

If Depth Score is high + shadow scores are high →
You feed this into Match Logic as a risk adjustment.

⭐ 11. How the OS Talks About Shadows (user-facing)

Important:
We do NOT label them to their face as “you’re a Manipulator.”

We do:

“You have a pattern of testing people emotionally when you feel unsafe.”
“You tend to disappear instead of explaining what’s wrong.”
“You rush into intensity, then feel trapped.”
“You shape-shift to avoid losing people, then feel unseen.”
“You hold quiet ledgers of everything you give.”
“You sometimes break what feels good because it feels unfamiliar.”

Shadows are translated into behaviors, not diagnoses.
