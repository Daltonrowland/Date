⭐ 1. THE PURPOSE (user-facing)

When entering the Emotional Vault, Season of Healing, Last Light, or Ex’s Anonymous, the app gently communicates:

“You never have to go deeper than you’re ready for.
Every question has a soft version, a skip option, and a gentle pace.”

Not one user feels exposed, cornered, or rushed.

⸻

⭐ 2. THE 5 TRAUMA-SAFE RULES (Foundation Framework)

You use FIVE rules across the entire system:

RULE 1 — No direct trauma prompts.

Never ask:
	•	“What traumatized you?”
	•	“What’s your biggest wound?”
	•	“Describe your pain.”

You ask:
	•	“What shaped you early?”
	•	“What moment taught you too much too fast?”
	•	“What part of your story still feels heavy sometimes?”

This protects the psyche.

⸻

RULE 2 — Always offer a soft version of every question.

Every deep question comes with a “gentle version”:

Example:
Main: “What did love teach you too early?”
Soft: “Tell me one small thing you learned about relationships growing up.”

Let THEM choose depth.

⸻

RULE 3 — Always provide a ‘Not ready’ option with praise.

Instead of Skip, you say:

“Not ready — that’s completely okay.”

And after they choose it:

“Thank you for honoring your pace.”

This makes avoidance feel empowered, not shameful.

⸻

RULE 4 — No chain-triggering.

Back-to-back heavy questions NEVER happen.

After a heavier prompt, the next one is light:
	•	Self-belief
	•	Identity
	•	Future hope
	•	Something calming
	•	Something neutral

This prevents emotional spirals.

⸻

RULE 5 — Stop if the user pauses too long.

If they hesitate (silence for 20–30 seconds), your system intervenes:

“Let’s take a breath.
We can return to this anytime.”

And Sanctuary opens Selah Mode.

You become the guardian of their pace.

⸻

⭐ 4. 3-TIER PROMPT SYSTEM (Trauma-Proof Question Design)

Zero pressure.
